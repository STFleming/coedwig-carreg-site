Session 3

“Have you been up to no good fighting them? Could you get me out of here? I will pay you well?” He says he

He is called Thinwhistle works for Mallowheart and was imprisoned by him for cooking something wrong.

In the cell next door Clement was kept in his dream. He was kept there with a Drune who was scratching away in corner. We enter and recognise elven script scratched into the wall – It reads “Mallow heart be curse” “Famine whistle be doubly cursed.” “Hound baker”

We take the skull and Barney asks the thin elf whether he knows who the skeleton is? He says he was an elf courtier who was apparently killed by Mallowheart as well.

In the room at the top right of the corridor Clement see’s an ornate room with a large table and 6 alcoves, each with a faded mural. There is a magical frost covering everything in the room. In one mural there is a portly fey noble with the inscription “Lord Slumberbone”. Clement sees beneath the table there is another mural. He heads over to a set of smashed doors, each with a carving of icy tree branches upon them. Pushing through the door clement sees the room is destroyed with crumbling plaster and guano everywhere, we think this must be the next of the creatures that attacked us from above. Exploring further the torch light reveals a dead body in the other room which we as a group approach to investigate. We see that the body is covered in puncture wounds and the body is drained of blood. Obolm investigates the body and finds an owl torque (worth 150gp) which we recognise to be a bad omen in Dolmenwood, alongside this there is a scroll sealed by a silver ring (eye shaped worth 750gp). Barney recognises it to be a scroll of Decipher, when he rummages through the body he finds a orange gem (50gp) that when touched he has a vision of a twitching twine coated stag corpse surrounded by trilithon stones “Ill omens at the withing stone, winter cometh”. We recognise the body to be that of a drune.

We return to the frosty room and as a group move the table, seeing the beautiful carved scenes on it of elves hunting. On one mural we see 3 bright red hounds (that appear to have been added after the fact) yipping at the feet of a slender elf with scraggly hair playing a tin whistle , below it says “Famine Whistle Wither”. In another mural Clement recognises an evil looking elf who is Mallowheart, either side of him there are two elves looking away from him with the inscription “Prince 7 Past Noon”. On another there is a slender elven woman with a mirror “lady silver so mirrored”. Another is a frost clad knight. Another has a statues woman holding a sword and shield called “whisper of candle light”.

We decide to investigate the sundial again, using the torch to shine on 7 past noon. We hear a grinding noise down the corridor and the noise of pan pipes grow loud. The frost rushes down the corridor towards Thinwhistle who approaches us, happy that he is let out and doubly happy that he doesn’t owe us anything for it. He marches past us and excitedly opens the door to Grognot.

Inside the room there is a 7 foot corrupted crook horn with knarled horns, wearing a weighty greasy fur cloak, his eyes glow and surrounding him there are noxious yellow candles that he breathes in deeply. In the corner there is a youthful human male who is staring into space twitching, he is bound with rope. Around the room there are smashed crates everywhere. The encounter begins…and grogot smashes into Thinwhistle.

While Fennic engages in melee. Barney tries to knock over the candles. Obolm checks out the human in the corner. Grend takes up position at the door shooting arrows into Grognot and with a lucky shot hits him right in the eye, dropping him to the floor. Thinwhistle looks shaken after the attack, and we use this as an opportunity to interrogate him. We ask him about the killing of the hounds and also about the location of the Whything Stones. He says that the cold princes “daughter” (a mortal) would meet at the Whything Stones, they are near to this location ( a few hexes north). He leaves, killing the monster barring the exit.

Barney finds a reinforced barred chest as well as a what appears to be a false wall. We push past it and find another room with high vaulted ceilings, it is cold with gentle snowfall going from the ceiling. In the middle there is a 10ft stone basin with stag skull carvings around the edge. Fennic leaps up onto the edge and sees a beautiful chalice beneath the ice. When hacking at the ice with his spear the chalice starts to disappear each time the ice is attacked.

In the other room Clement has left the dungeon with the normal human who is starting to recover.

Looking at the murals around the edges we can make out the elves killing humans and draining their blood into a lake. Clement returns and tells us that he recognises the chalice from his dream – a person was chasing him trying to get his blood. Clement drops his blood into the basin and the ice melts, her reaches in and grabs the icy chalice. Within the chalice he sees some strange liquid within the chalice (2000gp) that pulsates with colours and smells like the smellers favourite drink. Barney searches grognot and in the process gets public lice, but he does find a note as well as the key for the chest nearby. Barney opens the chest and we hear a mechanism disengaging. Inside we find two big sacks (1 filled with 200silver pieces, 1 500gp, a pair of amber studded bracelets (750gp each = 1500gp), there is also a note in Grends language.

The normal human awakes and he tells us he is from Dreg, a settlement nearby. He says brother Hogbeards zealots captured him after he was caught stealing. He says that Dreg has become lawless and these zealots have been taking the law into their own hands. He escaped by punching them and escaping into the forest, only to be found by the Breggles and taken down here.

We decide to camp by the fire for the night before then heading to Dreg the next day.
